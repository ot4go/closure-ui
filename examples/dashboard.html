Dashboard shell demo

A fixed header with a hamburger and free tools, a collapsible side nav (collapse it with ☰ — below 880px wide it overlays with a scrim), and this scrollable client area. The whole page is pure HTML: everything you see is the component's built-in default styling.

The first nav group works fully offline: those items switch local <dash-panel> regions, preserving their DOM state. The second group fetches content, so it needs the page served over HTTP: run go run . in examples/server and open http://localhost:8099/dashboard.html (from file:// those items fire dash-fetch-error, logged in the Events panel).

The side nav also carries free content: a native mini form (submits like any form — the closure machinery is opt-in, not assumed) and a plain button that sets a badge on the Tabs item.

Form panel with dirty guard — ● unsaved changes

This panel holds a <target-closure> with a form. Its nav item has refresh, so re-entering normally re-fetches fragments/form.html (resetting the form) — unless the closure is dirty: type something below, switch to another section and come back. The refetch is skipped (dash-dirty-skip in the Events panel), your text is still here, and the «unsaved changes» badge (dirty-show) stays on. Leave the field untouched and every visit resets the panel.
